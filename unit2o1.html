1.5 - Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites.

Copyright is when someone creates a piece of original work. As soon as an image is created it has copyright and the person who created it owns the rights to that image.
Only the owner has the right of the work can use the work in different capacities like selling it, displaying the work in different formats, or recreating said work.
If work is not copyrighted before uploaded to a website it could cause and issue amongst the content owner and the website creator. Which means that the content would have to be take down.

"Copyright refers to the legal protection of original works of authorship. This includes literary, written, dramatic, artistic, musical, and other types of creative expression.
It does not include industrial or technological inventions, although there are jurisdictions that consider computer software a creative work capable of copyright.
Sometimes copyright protection overlaps with other types of intellectual property.
For example, an online game may contain storylines and music appropriate for copyright registration, but also contains processes that need protection under patent law.
Websites contain written material, graphics, business logos, and other visual and sound elements. This firmly categorizes them as copyrightable material."

1.6 - Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can over come them.

You could get your website to cater to people will different levels of visual impairment.
Someone might be colour blind so you could have a feature which changes the colours on the webpage.
If their eye sight is bad then you could make sure that the website has larger fonts.
If they're close to being blind or have terrible eyesight the website could have click features, which is a noise that could come up whenever their mouse hovers over a word with a hyperlink.

If someone did not speak english and your website was in english, you could add a translation button to the website so it changes the text of the language that they speak. This is not a disability, however it is something that you could incorporate.
